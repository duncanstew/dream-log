Inducing Lucid Dreaming

Lucid dreaming can occur organically, but it can also be induced with some effort. Below are some research-supported methods that will help you lucid dream more frequently.

Wake-Induced Lucid Dreaming

  • Step 1
  • Choose a dream that you have often, and can easily recreate in your mind.
  • Step 2
  • Lie completely still, focusing on the weight of your body sinking into your mattress. Allow your mind to empty itself and observe the blackness of your closed eyelids. Observe, but don’t interact with the thoughts in your mind. Try to ignore your inner monologue and focus on relaxation.
  • Step 3
  • After enough time you may experience hypnagogia, which is a transitional stage from wakefulness to sleep. Hypnogagia can be characterized by colors, patterns, and audio hallucinations. You might also experience sleep paralysis, but don’t fight it! You are very close to entering a Wake Induced Lucid Dream.
  • Step 4
  • While in this transition stage, visualize the dream you want to enter. Pay attention to key details, or try to walk around. With enough practice, you will enter a lucid dream!

Mnemonic Induced Lucid Dream

  • Step 1
  • As you fall asleep, repeat a short mantra in your head, such as “Next time I’m dreaming, I will remember that I’m dreaming.”
  • Step 2
  • As you repeat your mantra, visualize what it means. Imagine yourself reenacting a recent dream and recognizing any dream signs or inconsistencies, like recurring characters or impossible physics. For example, if you dreamed about flying last night, practice realizing that flying means you must be dreaming.
  • Step 3
  • Be sure that the last thought you have before falling asleep is the intention to recognize a dream sign. When you enter a dream, you are more likely to notice those signs and become lucid.
  • Step 4
  • If you didn't have a dream, try repeating the mantra "next time I am dreaming, I will remember I'm dreaming."

Wake Back To Bed

  • Step 1
  • Set an alarm for approximately four to six hours after you go to bed, then fall asleep normally.
  • Step 2
  • After the alarm wakes you, write down any dreams you remember. Stay up for 30 to 60 minutes and complete a relaxing activity that doesn’t include electronics.
  • Step 3
  • While you fall back to sleep, practice MILD or WILD. Replay your most recent dream in your mind, and search for any inconsistencies that will help you realize you are dreaming.
  • Step 4
  • If you didn't have a dream, try repeating the mantra "Next time I am dreaming, I will remember I'm dreaming."
